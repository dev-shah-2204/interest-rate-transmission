Certificate of Deposit: Deposits of fixed-term issued by banks, providing a specified interest and maturity date; low risk with certificate negotiability unavailable.

Commercial Paper: A kind of unsecured promissory notes that are issued by corporations to raise working capital. This has a usual maturity of up to 270 days.

365-Day Bill Yield (percent): An annualized yield on a 365-day government treasury bill. This reflects the return as a percentage of face value.

Loan: An amount borrowed from banks or other financial institutions at an agreed rate of interest. The amount is repayable over a defined term.

Fixed Term Deposit (>1 year): A deposit which has tenured longer than one year, in return offering more lucrative rates with reduced liquidity.

Saving Deposit: A deposit account with banking that ascertains interest but makes provision for unlimited access to funds to allow depositors to save more.

Call Rate (% per annum): The rate at which banks lend and borrow overnight interbank credit facilities for day-to-day liquidity requirements.
